The Resistance

The Resistance refers to the group of diverse people fighting explicitly the United States government in a bout to gain rights for minority groups including but not limited to black people, homosexuals, and women. They also seek a less corrupt democracy with less violence against political opposition parties including the violence again Communists.

The Resistance was founded unofficially in 1956 by the Leader. They, along with a close friend named Richard Donald, gathered five other members to begin their planing. The goal? Root out fascism. The means? Protests and riots at first. Political violence second. They developed an underground network for moving information and organizing people for the cause. Nearly every protest and riot from the West coast to the East had, in some way, can be linked back to the Resistance.

The Resistance is funded mostly through anonymous sponsors and donations from members. This usually leaves things spread rather thin, though most members are accustomed to working with very little. One source of resources comes from the [USSR]. They saw this as an opportunity to spread their form of communism within American territory without drawing too much attention. They donate food, water, weapons, money, and other miscellaneous gear that may come in handy. The equipment is hidden around the country in abandoned buildings, in peoples attics, and where ever else people could think to hid it. Some crates are even buried under farmland waiting for the right moment.

Symbolism

The primary symbol of The Resistance is an American flag with the top left and bottom right corner painted black.
On clothing, a clenched raised fist is sometimes sewn onto jackets when a member wishes to be recognized. Colors vary.

Espionage

In 1957, Leader put [Robert Irwine] in charge of communications and espionage. Thanks to his service as an ex-communications officer during WWII he was able to put together a team and begin networking all the major resistance facilities while also setting up listening posts to queue them in on police activity.
