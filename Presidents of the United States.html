Presidents of the United States

Presidents in the United States has always been seen as having great power. The are the commander in chief of the US military. They can sign bills into law. They are expected to lead the country in the right direction. In this setting, presidential elections don't pan out the way you'd expect. The formation of the Dixiecrat party in 1948 gain more popularity than projected, this in part do to backing from German officials. It split the popular vote for Republican candidate Thomas E. Dewey and the Dixiecrat Strom Thurmond, each obtaining about 30% of the popular vote and Thurmond taking many souther state electoral votes.

Many Americans thought Truman wasn't hard enough on the spread of Communism and that his desire for Civil rights was against what many stood for. Many southern states started flipping Dixiecrat while more moderate states turned to the Republican party.
